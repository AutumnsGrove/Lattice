# What is Canopy?

[[canopy|Canopy]] is [[your-grove|Grove]]'s public directory — a place where wanderers can browse and discover groves across the community.

Think of it as the treetop view of the forest. From up here, you can see who's out there and what they're creating.

## How it works

When you enable [[canopy|Canopy]] visibility in your settings, your [[your-grove|grove]] appears in the public directory at [grove.place/canopy](https://grove.place/canopy). Other wanderers can browse by category, see your banner, and visit your site.

**Key points:**
- Appearing in the [[canopy|Canopy]] is always opt-in
- You choose which categories describe your grove
- You can set a short banner message to introduce yourself
- You can leave at any time by toggling visibility off

## Categories

When you join the [[canopy|Canopy]], you pick categories that describe what your grove is about — things like personal blog, creative writing, tech, art, or community. This helps other wanderers find groves that match their interests.

You can select multiple categories and change them whenever you like.

## Your banner

Your banner is a short line that appears alongside your grove in the directory. It's your chance to say hello in a sentence — what you write about, what drives you, or just a warm greeting.

Keep it authentic. The best banners sound like you.

## Privacy and control

The [[canopy|Canopy]] only shows what you choose to share:

| What's shown | Details |
|---|---|
| Grove name | Your site title |
| Banner | The message you write |
| Categories | The ones you select |
| Profile photo | If you've set one |

Your posts, pages, and private content are never exposed through the directory. The [[canopy|Canopy]] is a signpost, not a window.

## Opting out

Changed your mind? Toggle [[canopy|Canopy]] visibility off in your settings and your grove disappears from the directory immediately. No waiting period, no questions asked.

Your grove keeps working exactly the same either way. The [[canopy|Canopy]] is purely about discoverability.

## Why Canopy exists

Building a blog can feel lonely. You put your thoughts out there and wonder if anyone's listening. The [[canopy|Canopy]] makes it easier for like-minded people to find each other — not through algorithms or engagement metrics, but through honest self-description and shared interests.

It's a directory in the oldest, most human sense: a list of neighbors, organized by what they care about.
