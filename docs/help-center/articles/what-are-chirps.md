# What are Chirps?

Two robins on a branch. One turns to the other and calls out. The other calls back. That's a chirp.

[[chirp|Chirps]] are Grove's private 1:1 messages. Quick, real-time conversations between you and one other [[wanderer|Wanderer]]. Not a group chat. Not a public feed. Just two people, talking.

## Why Chirps exist

[[meadow|Meadow]] is the open field. [[reeds|Reeds]] are the comments beneath your posts. [[ivy|Ivy]] is email — proper, encrypted, formal. But sometimes you just want to say "hey, did you see this?" to a friend without writing a letter about it.

Chirps fill the space between public and formal. They're the quick back-and-forth that happens when you're comfortable with someone. The "oh my god you have to hear this" energy. The casual catch-up. The shared photo with no caption needed.

## How it works

Chirps live inside [[arbor|Arbor]], your admin dashboard. Open your chirps and you'll see your conversations listed by most recent activity.

**Sending a chirp** — Pick a friend, type your message, hit send. They'll see it instantly if they're online, or it'll be waiting when they come back.

**Images** — Share photos alongside text. Drop an image into the conversation and it's there.

**Typing indicators** — When your friend is composing a reply, you'll see it. Like watching the other robin tilt its head before it chirps back.

**Read receipts** — Know when your message has been seen. No ambiguity.

**Retraction** — Said something you didn't mean? Pull it back before it settles. Messages can be retracted — the words disappear, leaving only a note that something was there.

## What this means for you

**Private by default.** Chirps are between you and one person. Nobody else sees them. No public inbox, no follower count on your conversations.

**Real-time.** Messages arrive instantly over WebSocket. No refreshing, no polling. The conversation flows naturally.

**Just two people.** Chirps are 1:1. This is intentional. Group conversations are a different kind of space — chirps are for the branch with room for two.

## The name

Users are robins. Robins chirp. A chirp is short, bright, unmistakable — one bird calling to another across the branches. Not a song performed for an audience. Just a quick, joyful exchange between friends.

> "I chirped you about that bloom."
> "Check your chirps — I sent photos."
> "We were chirping all night."
