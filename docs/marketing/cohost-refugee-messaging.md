# Cohost Refugee Messaging

*Reaching the displaced community from "the last meaningful refuge of the queer internet"*

---

## Context

**What happened:** Cohost shut down January 12, 2025, after announcing in September 2024 they couldn't sustain operations. They had ~30K monthly active users but only 2,630 paying subscribers â€” not enough to survive.

**Who they were:** Cohost was described as "the last meaningful refuge of the queer internet" â€” a place where queer and marginalized users could be "unashamedly weird and uncompromisingly themselves."

**The opportunity:** It's been ~6 months. They're still looking for home. Grove is what they were looking for.

Cohost users called it a "refuge." Grove is literally positioned as a "sanctuary." This language resonates.

### Specific Outreach

**Do:**
- Engage genuinely in discussions about missing Cohost
- Share Grove when relevant (not spam)
- Acknowledge the grief (people genuinely mourned Cohost)

**Don't:**
- Spam "come to Grove!" on every Cohost memorial post
- Pretend to be a community member if you weren't on Cohost
- Dismiss what made Cohost special
- Promise you'll never shut down (you can't guarantee that)

## Tone Guidelines

**Do:**
- Acknowledge the grief (Cohost closing was genuinely sad)
- Be humble (you're new, they had something special)
- Be honest about being a business (you charge money, that's why you'll survive)
- Celebrate what made Cohost good (don't just use them for marketing)

**Don't:**
- Be ghoulish ("Cohost is dead, come to us!")
- Overpromise ("We'll never shut down!")
- Dismiss concerns ("That won't happen to us")
- Pretend you're identical (Grove is different, own that)
