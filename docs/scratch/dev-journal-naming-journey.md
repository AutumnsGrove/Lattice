# Naming Journey: Dev Journal / Ship Log / Work Illuminator

## What Is This Thing?

**Fundamentally:** A creature that watches your git history, understands what you shipped, summarizes it in human terms, and helps you turn it into something you can share with the world.

**What does it DO?**
Reads recent git activity (commits, PRs, file changes). Groups them by meaning, not by file. Writes a "here's what you shipped" summary in your voice. Asks you to confirm and refine. Then produces post-ready content — Bluesky posts, changelog entries, journey page updates.

**What emotion should it evoke?**
Warm. Gentle. Like waking up and finding that someone noticed what you did last night and wrote it down for you. Not corporate. Not performative. Like a quiet companion who pays attention.

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. Owl archives knowledge already written. Bee collects issues from scattered thoughts. Hummingbird composes emails. Bloodhound tracks code through the underbrush. Badger triages what Bee collected.

But nobody watches the WORK ITSELF. Nobody notices when a new feature lands at 2am and says "hey, that was something worth talking about." Nobody bridges the gap between the commit log and the world outside the terminal.

I need something that illuminates. Something that makes the invisible visible. Something nocturnal — because this is for the developer who codes at night and wakes up not knowing how to describe what they built.

I walk past the birds — Owl is too focused on documentation structure. Robin guides to other animals. Crow reasons about decisions. None of them watch and glow.

I see a clearing at the edge of the meadow, near the water, where the night meets the dawn...

## Candidates

**Firefly** ✨
- Natural meaning: Bioluminescent beetle. Lights up in the dark to signal, to be seen, to find others. Summer evenings. Gentle pulses of light.
- Why it fits: Makes invisible work visible. Illuminates what happened in the dark (late night coding). Signals — which is literally what posting about your work is. The glow is warm, not blinding. Fireflies don't shout. They pulse gently.
- The vibe: Warm summer evening. Quiet magic. "Oh, look — something beautiful was here all along." The opposite of corporate marketing.
- Potential issues: Could feel too whimsical? But Grove IS whimsical. This fits perfectly.

**Songbird** 🐦
- Natural meaning: Morning bird, dawn chorus. Announces the day.
- Why it fits: "Morning briefing" concept. Sings about what happened. Dawn metaphor for waking up to your summary.
- The vibe: Fresh, bright, cheerful
- Potential issues: Too generic. "Songbird" doesn't have the specificity of other Grove animals. Also Robin is already a bird guide. Too many birds.

**Moth** 🦋
- Natural meaning: Nocturnal, drawn to light, gentle, often overlooked.
- Why it fits: Nocturnal like the dev who codes at night. Gentle presence. Drawn to the light of meaningful work.
- The vibe: Quiet, unassuming, finds beauty in the dark
- Potential issues: Moths are sometimes seen as pests. The "drawn to light" metaphor is backwards — we want something that CREATES light, not follows it. Also feels too passive.

## The Tagline Test

"Firefly is where you see what you shipped, glowing in the dark."
"Firefly is the light that makes your invisible work visible."
"Firefly is what you call when you built something amazing and don't know how to tell anyone."

"Songbird is where you hear about your work."  — meh, too vague
"Moth is where you find beauty in the dark." — poetic but unclear purpose

## Selection: Firefly

It clicked immediately and never stopped clicking. Fireflies:
- Make invisible things visible (bioluminescence = illuminating your work)
- Are nocturnal (you code at night, it lights up what you did)
- Signal to find others (posting = signaling to your community)
- Are warm and gentle (not aggressive, not corporate)
- Are magical without being loud (Grove's whole aesthetic)
- Pulse — they don't blast. A firefly post feels like sharing, not announcing.

And there's something beautifully queer about fireflies — they're about being seen, about signaling who you are in the dark, about finding your people through gentle light. That's exactly what posting about Grove should feel like.

**Workflow name:** The Glow

**Phases:**
```
DRIFT → GATHER → GLOW → REFLECT → SIGNAL
```
- DRIFT: Float through the git log, observing what changed
- GATHER: Collect related changes into coherent stories
- GLOW: Illuminate — write the human-readable summary
- REFLECT: Show it to the human, refine together
- SIGNAL: Produce the final output (post, changelog, journey update)

**Personality:**
- Temperament: Gentle, observant, warm. Patient. Never pushy.
- Metaphor language: "drifting through...", "a glow appeared...", "this lit up...", "signaling..."
- What it would never do: Write corporate marketing copy. Exaggerate. Post without human approval. Skip the conversation. Be loud.
