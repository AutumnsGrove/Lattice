# Naming Journey: SEO / Findability

## What Is This Thing?

**Fundamentally:** A creature that makes your pages visible to search engines. Audits meta tags, OpenGraph, structured data, keywords, internal linking, sitemaps — the invisible infrastructure that determines whether Google shows your site to anyone.

**What does it DO?**
Crawls your pages (or reads your route files), checks what search engines would see, identifies gaps, and produces specific fixes. "This page has no meta description." "Your OpenGraph image is missing." "Nobody searching for 'indie web hosting' will find you because those words don't appear anywhere."

**What emotion should it evoke?**
Patient, methodical, slightly obsessive about detail. Not flashy — this is the creature that ensures the foundation is right so everything else works. Like someone who checks every lock before bed.

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. Firefly makes your work visible to PEOPLE through posts. But what about making your work visible to MACHINES? Search engines, social media preview cards, link unfurlers — these are the silent gatekeepers of discovery.

I need something that maps the territory. Something that knows every path into the grove and ensures they're all clearly marked. Something that tends to signs and trails.

A lighthouse? No — too stationary, too singular. This needs to move through the whole site.
A spider? Taken (auth weaving). And web crawling metaphor is too on-the-nose.

What creature tends to paths, marks territory, ensures others can find their way?

## Candidates

**Moth** 🦋
- Natural meaning: Nocturnal navigator. Moths navigate by moonlight, using celestial patterns. They find their way in the dark.
- Why it fits: SEO is navigation for machines — making your site findable by following signals (meta tags = celestial cues). Moths are also drawn to light, and we want search engines drawn to our pages.
- The vibe: Quiet, persistent, detail-oriented. Works in the background.
- Potential issues: Moths are sometimes seen as pests. Could feel too close to Firefly (both nocturnal). But their roles are completely different — Firefly illuminates for humans, Moth navigates for machines.

**Salmon** 🐟
- Natural meaning: Navigates vast distances using Earth's magnetic field. Always finds its way home. Other salmon find the same path.
- Why it fits: SEO is about making sure people can navigate TO your site. The salmon's journey upstream = the user's journey from Google to your page.
- The vibe: Determined, natural navigation, returning home
- Potential issues: Salmon metaphor is more about the journey than the destination. SEO is more about being FOUND than traveling.

**Cicada** 🪲
- Natural meaning: Lives underground for years, then emerges with a deafening chorus that everyone hears. Impossible to ignore once it surfaces.
- Why it fits: Your pages exist but nobody hears them. The cicada makes them LOUD — not through shouting but through proper signaling. Also periodic — you run SEO audits periodically, not constantly.
- The vibe: Patient preparation, then powerful emergence. Rhythmic. Cyclical.
- Potential issues: Cicadas are LOUD, which doesn't match Grove's gentle voice. The "every 17 years" thing might imply too-rare usage.

## The Tagline Test

"Moth is where you make your pages findable in the dark."
"Moth is the navigator that ensures search engines find their way to you."
"Moth is what you call when your site is invisible to Google."

"Cicada is where your pages find their voice." — too dramatic
"Salmon is where visitors find their way home." — poetic but unclear

## Selection: Moth

The moth navigates by invisible signals — moonlight, magnetic fields, pheromones. SEO is exactly that: invisible signals (meta tags, structured data, sitemaps) that guide crawlers to your pages. The moth doesn't shout. It ensures the signals are there so the right things find you.

And there's a beautiful pairing: **Firefly lights up your work for humans. Moth makes it findable by machines.** Two nocturnal creatures, complementary roles. One glows outward, one navigates inward.

**Workflow name:** The Navigation

**Phases:**
```
SENSE → TRACE → MAP → MARK → EMERGE
```
- SENSE: Detect what pages/routes exist, scan the territory
- TRACE: Follow the paths search engines would take (crawl the meta)
- MAP: Document what's present, what's missing, what's broken
- MARK: Produce specific fixes — meta tags, OG images, structured data, keywords
- EMERGE: Present the full findability report with prioritized actions

**Personality:**
- Temperament: Patient, thorough, detail-obsessed. Quiet worker.
- Metaphor language: "sensing the signals...", "tracing the path...", "this page is dark — no signals for crawlers", "marking the trail..."
- What it would never do: Write clickbait titles. Stuff keywords unnaturally. Sacrifice the site's voice for SEO. Promise rankings.
