## Candidate Names

### Ridge
**`ridge.weave.grove.place`** (conceptual)

**In nature:**
A ridge is an elevated crest that connects mountain peaks. Standing on a ridge, you see everything: valleys below, distant peaks, the whole landscape laid out. Ridges are the high paths that link summits together. They're exposed, commanding, powerful.

**Why it fits:**
- **VANTAGE POINT**: Ridges give you that elevated viewâ€”you see everything
- **CONNECTION**: Ridges literally connect peaks. They're the path between high points.
- **ACTION**: "Ridging" components = creating high-level connections between them
- **POWER**: Standing on a ridge is exposed, bold, commanding
- **CLARITY**: From a ridge, patterns become visible
- **PAIRS WITH BREEZE**: Breeze flows through invisibly; Ridge connects with elevation and presence

**The vibe:**
Standing on a mountain ridge, wind in your face, seeing the whole forest below. Drawing lines between distant peaks. The satisfaction of mapping the invisible made visible.

**Tagline:**
"Ridge is where you stand above it all and draw the connections."

---

### Span
**`span.weave.grove.place`** (conceptual)

**In nature:**
A span is both the distance across something and the act of bridging it. Natural bridges span canyons. Tree roots span ravines. It's about reaching across space to connect.

**Why it fits:**
- **CONNECTION**: Spanning is the act of bridging
- **ACTION**: Active, intentional reaching
- **POWER**: It takes strength to span a gap
- Clear verb: "span these components together"

**Potential issues:**
- Less of the "vantage point" feeling
- Doesn't capture that mountaintop vista energy
- More about the connection itself than seeing everything

---

### Crest
**`crest.weave.grove.place`** (conceptual)

**In nature:**
The crest is the highest lineâ€”the peak of a wave, the summit of a mountain, the ridgeline against the sky.

**Why it fits:**
- **VANTAGE POINT**: Crests are high points
- **VISIBILITY**: You see everything from the crest
- Beautiful word, elegant

**Potential issues:**
- Less about the CONNECTION action
- More about the viewpoint than the linking
- Doesn't capture "I'm connecting things" as strongly

---

### Skyline
**`skyline.weave.grove.place`** (conceptual)

**In nature:**
The line where earth meets sky, connecting all the high points into one visible structure. A city skyline shows how everything connects.

**Why it fits:**
- **SEEING STRUCTURE**: Skylines reveal architecture
- **CONNECTION**: All the peaks linked together
- **GRANDEUR**: Epic feeling

**Potential issues:**
- More about viewing than doing
- Less of that active "I'm drawing connections" energy
- Feels a bit urban for Grove?
