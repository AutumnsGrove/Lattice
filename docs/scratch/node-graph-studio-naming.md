## Candidates

### 1. Bower

**A shelter made of interwoven branches, or a bowerbird's elaborate structure**

```

```

- Bowerbirds create intricate structures from collected materials
- It's about arrangement, composition, visual appeal
- A bower is *architecture that grows*
- "Open the Bower" â€” enter the composition studio
- "Build a Bower" â€” create an interconnected structure

**Tagline:** *"Arrange with intention. Connect with care."*

**Potential issues:**
- Some people don't know what a bower is
- Might sound too "sheltery" when it's really about creation

---

### 2. Canopy

**Where branches interlock overhead**

```
grove.place/canopy
```

- The upper layer of the forest where trees connect
- A network of interlocking branches
- You look up and see the pattern
- "Open the Canopy" â€” see the connections

**Tagline:** *"Where branches meet."*

**Potential issues:**
- Sounds more observational than creative
- Canopy is a view, not a tool

---

### 3. Weave

**The action of interlacing threads**

```

```

- Simple, clear, active verb
- "Weave your scene together"

**Tagline:** *"Weave your world."*

**Potential issues:**
- More textile than nature
- Already using Loom metaphor for D1 batching

### 4. Tendril

**The reaching, grasping part of a climbing vine**


- Tendrils reach out and connect

**Potential issues:**
- Might work better as the name for CONNECTIONS rather than the STUDIO
- "Tendril Studio" sounds a bit odd

### 5. Separate Names: Sway + Fern

## The Choice: Bower

After walking through, **Bower** feels right.

A bower is:
- Intentionally created (you build it)
- Made of collected materials (your assets)
- Arranged with care (grid placement)
- A structure of relationships (connections)
- Both functional and beautiful

The bowerbird connection is perfect:
- Bowerbirds are famous for their elaborate constructions
- They collect items and arrange them precisely
- The bower is both architecture and art
- It's about composition and connection

**Bower** unifies animation and diagrams:
- Animation: *"Build a Bower of swaying vines"*
- Diagrams: *"Build a Bower of connected ideas"*

And it fits the Grove ecosystem:
- Natural metaphor
- About creation, not just observation
- Warm, intentional, crafted
- Not directly about trees â€” about what happens in the forest

## Final Decision: Weave

*Updated after further discussion*

After living with "Bower" for a few hours, we pivoted to **Weave**.

### Why the change?

**Bower** felt right initially but had friction:
- It's a noun â€” you *use* a bower, you don't *bower* something
- Requires explaining what a bowerbird is
- More about the result than the action

**Weave** won because:
- It's a **verb** â€” "weave your scene together" is an action
- Immediately intuitive â€” everyone knows what weaving means
- The metaphor still works: threads connecting nodes, patterns emerging
- "Weave" as the studio, "Threads" as the connections â€” natural pairing

### Final naming structure

| Public Name | Internal Name | Icon | Purpose |
|-------------|---------------|------|---------|
| **Weave** | GroveWeave | `spline-pointer` | Unified composition studio |
| **Sway** | GroveSway | `waves` | Animation sub-mode |
| **Fern** | GroveFern | `waypoints` | Diagram sub-mode |
| **Threads** | GroveThreads | `route` | Connections between nodes |

### The tagline

> *Weave your world together.*

Active, warm, clear. Fits Grove's voice perfectly.
