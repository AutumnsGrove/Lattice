# User Naming Journey

> What do we call the people who call the Grove home?

---

## The Problem

Right now we have:
- Users = Trees (established metaphor)
- But "trees" doesn't work as something you call people
- "Members" feels corporate, subscription-y, cold
- "Grovers" sounds like Sesame Street
- "Residents" lacks the nature connection

We need:
1. **Individual name** - What do you call ONE person?
2. **Collective name** - What do you call ALL of them together?
3. **Action/belonging language** - When someone subscribes, what happens? What ARE they now?

## What IS a User in Grove?

Not a customer. Not a subscriber. Not a member.

They're someone who **planted themselves here**.
They chose to put down roots.
They're growing in community with others.

**What they ARE:**
- A tree among trees
- Someone who planted
- Someone who's growing
- Someone rooted here

**What they DO:**
- They grow (write, create)
- They reach (connect with others)
- They shelter (provide value to readers)
- They root (commit to this place)

## Candidate Directions

### Direction 1: The Rooted Ones
Focus on putting down roots, commitment to place.

- **Rooted** - "The Rooted" as collective, "I'm rooted at Grove"
- **Roots** - "Fellow roots" - but sounds weird
- **Planted** - "The Planted" - too past-tense?

### Direction 2: Growth & Living
Focus on being alive, growing.

- **Saplings** - New users are saplings, established ones are... what?
- **Growers** - "We're all growers here"
- **Growing** - "The Growing" (feels incomplete)

### Direction 3: Forest Community Words
Real terms for tree communities.

- **Stand** - A group of trees. "The Stand" for collective?
- **Grove** - Already used for the place itself
- **Canopy** - "Under the canopy" for everyone together?

### Direction 4: What Trees Do Together
How trees interact.

- **Interlaced** - Roots interlock, branches interweave
- **Kindred** - Trees of the same kind, related
- **Kin** - Simple, warm, familial

### Direction 5: Seasonal/Cyclical
Trees are defined by seasons.

- **Perennials** - We come back, we endure
- **Evergreens** - We stay, we persist
- **Deciduous** - No, this means we lose our leaves...

### Direction 6: The Forest Floor
What grows beneath and between.

- **Understory** - The smaller plants beneath the canopy
- **Groundcover** - Feels too small
- **Undergrowth** - Negative connotation

### Direction 7: Shelter & Home
Trees as homes, as places of safety.

- **Dwellers** - "Grove dwellers"
- **Denizens** - "Denizens of the grove" - too formal?
- **Keepers** - "Grovekeepers" - but that's more like admins
