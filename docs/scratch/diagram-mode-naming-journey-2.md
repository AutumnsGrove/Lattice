## Testing: Frost

**What it means in nature:**
Ice crystals that form on cold surfaces, creating intricate geometric patterns. Most visible on glass, where the crystallization follows natural mathematical rules, creating branches and connections that reveal invisible structure.

**Why it fits diagrams:**
Diagrams are about making invisible architecture visible. Frost does exactly thisâ€”it crystallizes invisible vapor into visible geometric patterns. On our glass cards, frost would etch the structure, showing connections and relationships with mathematical precision.

**The vibe:**
Clear, crisp, structural, revealing. Not cold or harshâ€”think of the beauty of frost on a window. Intricate, geometric, intentional.

**How it pairs with Breeze:**
Perfect contrast. Breeze is warm air moving through space. Frost is crystallized structure appearing on surfaces. One flows, one reveals. One is movement, one is architecture.

**Tagline options:**
- "Where structure crystallizes"
- "Making architecture visible"
- "Patterns that reveal"
