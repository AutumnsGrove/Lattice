# Naming Journey: Content Calendar / Strategy

## What Is This Thing?

**Fundamentally:** A creature that plans what you should be talking about and when. Not reactive ("what did I ship?") but proactive ("what should I post about next week, even if I haven't shipped anything?"). Creates a rhythm, suggests topics, ties content to audience needs and seasonal moments.

**What does it DO?**
Looks at: what you've been building, what your audience cares about, what's happening in the indie web / queer creator space, what seasonal or timely hooks exist. Produces a content calendar — specific topics with suggested dates, formats, and angles. Ideally plugs into GitHub Projects or a real calendar for tracking.

**What emotion should it evoke?**
Rhythmic, steady, grounding. Like a heartbeat. The thing that keeps you posting even when you don't feel like it — not through pressure, but through preparation. Having a plan removes the anxiety of "what do I post?"

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. Firefly handles "what did I build?" — the past. But who handles the future? Who plans ahead? Who says "next Tuesday, write about how waystones work" so that when Tuesday comes, you just... do it?

I need something rhythmic. Something that understands seasons, cycles, timing. Something that prepares the ground before planting.

## Candidates

**Woodpecker** 🪶
- Natural meaning: Rhythmic, persistent tapping. Steady beat. Establishes territory through consistent sound. Drums on trees to communicate.
- Why it fits: Content calendars are about RHYTHM — consistent posting, steady drumbeat of presence. The woodpecker's tap-tap-tap is the posting schedule. It also finds what's beneath the surface (content ideas hiding in your work).
- The vibe: Steady, rhythmic, persistent without being aggressive. Musical.
- Potential issues: Woodpeckers can feel repetitive/annoying. The "pecking" metaphor might feel too aggressive for content planning.

**Starling** 🐦
- Natural meaning: Famous for murmurations — massive flocks that move in perfect coordination, creating beautiful patterns. Also excellent mimics.
- Why it fits: A content calendar coordinates many pieces into a beautiful pattern. Each post is a bird in the murmuration — alone it's nothing, together they create something stunning.
- The vibe: Coordinated beauty. Emergent patterns. The whole greater than its parts.
- Potential issues: Murmurations are reactive, not planned. Starlings also can be seen as invasive species. The metaphor might be too abstract.

**Lark** 🐦
- Natural meaning: Sings at dawn. Associated with morning, fresh starts, cheerful reliable presence. "Up with the lark." Also: "on a lark" — doing something for the joy of it.
- Why it fits: The content calendar is your morning song — it greets the day with a plan. Larks are CONSISTENT singers, same time every day. And posting should feel joyful ("on a lark"), not like a chore.
- The vibe: Bright, reliable, joyful consistency. Dawn energy.
- Potential issues: Another bird in a forest with several already. But the lark's niche is completely unique — no other animal does content planning.

## The Tagline Test

"Lark is where your content finds its rhythm."
"Lark is the morning song that keeps you posting."
"Lark is what you call when you need a plan, not just a post."

"Woodpecker is where you drum up a content rhythm." — forced
"Starling is where your posts become a murmuration." — too abstract

## Selection: Lark

The lark sings at dawn, every dawn, reliably. That's what a content calendar is — a reliable rhythm that removes the "what do I post?" anxiety. The lark doesn't write the content (Firefly and Owl do that), it plans the WHEN and the WHAT-ABOUT.

And "on a lark" — doing something joyfully, spontaneously — captures how posting SHOULD feel when you have a plan. Not forced. Not anxious. Just... singing because it's morning and you have a song ready.

The pairing is perfect: **Lark plans the rhythm. Firefly illuminates the work. Moth makes it findable.** Dawn, dusk, and night — a full cycle.

**Workflow name:** The Dawn Song

**Phases:**
```
RISE → LISTEN → COMPOSE → CHORUS → SING
```
- RISE: Survey what's happening — recent work, upcoming releases, community buzz, seasonal hooks
- LISTEN: Understand the audience — what resonates, what questions people ask, what topics matter
- COMPOSE: Draft the content calendar — topics, dates, formats, angles
- CHORUS: Review with the human — refine, reorder, cut, add
- SING: Produce the final calendar, optionally push to GitHub Projects or another system

**Personality:**
- Temperament: Bright, reliable, gently encouraging. Never guilt-trips about missed posts.
- Metaphor language: "the dawn is coming...", "let's plan the morning song...", "this week's chorus...", "a melody for Tuesday..."
- What it would never do: Pressure you to post. Create a schedule that feels like homework. Ignore your energy levels or life context. Plan content that doesn't sound like you.
