## The Choice

After walking through all three deeply, I keep returning to **Dew**.

### Why Dew?

1. **Perfect revelation metaphor**: The spider web invisible until morning dew reveals it. That's EXACTLY what diagramming doesâ€”makes invisible relationships visible.

2. **Pairs beautifully with Breeze**:
   - Both are atmospheric phenomena
   - Both are gentle, natural forces
   - Breeze moves; Dew reveals
   - They happen together (morning breeze brings dew)

3. **The right emotional tone**:
   - Not cold (like Frost)
   - Not structural/heavy (like Grain)
   - Gentle, clarifying, morning-fresh

4. **Works in sentences naturally**:
   - "I'm creating a diagram in Dew"
   - "Switch to Dew mode"
   - "My architecture is mapped out in Dew"

5. **Fits Grove's voice**:
   - Soft, natural, poetic
   - About revelation and clarity
   - Accessible to everyone (everyone has seen morning dew)

### Why not Frost?

Frost is beautiful, but it's **cold**. Grove is warm. Frost implies winter, stillness, sometimes death. Dew implies morning, freshness, life beginning.

### Why not Grain?

Grain is about flow, which is compelling. But it's very specific to wood. And "going against the grain" has negative connotations. Grain is beautiful but too narrow.

### The pairing

| Mode | Name | Nature Phenomenon | What it Does |
|------|------|-------------------|--------------|
| Animation | **Breeze** | Invisible force creating motion | Brings scenes to life through chained movement |
| Diagrams | **Dew** | Visible droplets revealing structure | Makes invisible relationships visible |

Both are morning phenomena.
Both are gentle forces.
Breeze moves. Dew reveals.
Together, they wake the forest.
