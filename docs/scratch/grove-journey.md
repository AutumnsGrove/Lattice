## The Question: Where Does Support Fit?

When you're in the grove and something's wrong...
When you're lost and Waystone isn't enough...
When you need to actually talk to someone...

What do you do in a forest?

- You don't shout into emptiness (that's Echo's problem)
- You don't just wander (Waystones help with that)
- You don't check a status board (that's Clearing)

You need... connection. Communication. Someone to hear you.

---

## What IS a support ticket in this world?

It's not a place you go.
It's not an object you send.

It's... a reaching out?
A call?
A signal?

In a forest, when you need help:
- You might light a fire (signal)
- You might find a ranger (person)
- You might leave a mark (communication)
- You might knock on a door (seeking connection)

But what if it's more organic than that?

---

## Things in nature that represent "reaching out for help":

- A bird call that gets answered
- Smoke rising (someone sees it)
- A tap on tree roots (mycelium carries signals)
- Sap flowing (the tree's own communication)
- Pollen carried on wind (reaches someone)

---

## Wait. What about the relationship between services?

Waystone = self-service help (trail markers, you follow them)
Clearing = status (you look and see what's happening)
??? = actual human connection (you reach out, someone responds)

The pattern seems to be:
- Some things are PLACES (Meadow, Nook, Pantry, Clearing)
- Some things are OBJECTS/PROCESSES (Amber, Patina, Bloom)
- Some things are FEATURES OF TREES (Foliage, Heartwood, Rings)
- Some things are CONNECTIONS (Ivy, Mycelium, Reeds)

Support tickets feel like... a CONNECTION.
Not a place you go, but a way of reaching someone.

---

## Connection-type names:

Ivy = email (climbing, connecting points)
Mycelium = underground network (invisible connections)
Reeds = comments (whispering together)

What connects you to the grove keeper?
What's the line between you and the person tending this forest?

## The Grove Keeper

There's someone tending this grove.
You're a tree. They're the one who checks on the trees.
When you need them... how do you reach them?

In real forests:
- Trees send chemical signals through roots
- Wounded trees release compounds that attract help
- Birds carry messages between areas

What if the support system is about...
REACHING the keeper?
SIGNALING that you need attention?

---

## New directions:

**Tap** - like tapping on roots, sending a signal through the network
**Signal** - too technical
**Beacon** - a light that says "I'm here, I need you"
**Call** - a bird call, expecting response
**Whistle** - a signal through the forest

Or what if it's about what the keeper uses?

**Rounds** - the keeper makes their rounds, checking on trees
**Watch** - the keeper's watch over the grove
**Tend** - to tend to something that needs care
