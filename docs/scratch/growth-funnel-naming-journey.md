# Naming Journey: Growth / Funnel Thinking

## What Is This Thing?

**Fundamentally:** A creature that thinks about the entire journey from "never heard of Grove" to "I use it every day and tell my friends." Not a marketer — more like a curious explorer who maps the territory between your audience and your product, finds the gaps, and suggests how to bridge them.

**What does it DO?**
Explores: Where do your potential users hang out? What are they searching for? What's their first impression of Grove? What makes them stay or leave? What would make them tell a friend? Produces insights and specific, actionable experiments — not a marketing plan, but "have you tried X? Here's why it might work."

**What emotion should it evoke?**
Curious, adventurous, slightly mischievous. Like a scout who goes ahead and comes back saying "I found a trail nobody's using — if we clear it, people would pour in." Energetic but strategic.

## Walking Through the Forest

I enter the grove. Firefly illuminates what you built. Moth makes it findable by machines. Lark plans the rhythm. But who goes OUT into the world and looks at things from the OTHER side? Who stands where your future users are standing and asks "how would I find Grove from here?"

I need an explorer. Something that ventures beyond the grove into the wider world, scouts the landscape, and comes back with intelligence. Not a predator (Fox, Panther). Not a builder. A scout — but Bloodhound scouts CODE, not people.

I need something that explores territory, finds paths, and — crucially — comes back to report.

## Candidates

**Magpie** 🐦
- Natural meaning: Incredibly curious. Collects shiny things. Highly intelligent. Goes everywhere, investigates everything, brings interesting objects back to the nest.
- Why it fits: Growth thinking IS curiosity — "what if we tried this? What about that audience? Look at what this competitor does!" The magpie goes out, finds shiny opportunities, and brings them back for you to evaluate.
- The vibe: Curious, energetic, slightly mischievous. "Look what I found!"
- Potential issues: "Magpie" can imply scattered attention. But with the right framing (curated curiosity, not random collecting), this is a strength.

**Otter** 🦦
- Natural meaning: Playful, intelligent, social. Uses tools. Explores waterways. Known for finding the most efficient paths through complex environments.
- Why it fits: Growth is about finding efficient paths from audience to product. Otters are also deeply social — they hold hands while sleeping, play together, share resources. Community-oriented.
- The vibe: Playful, clever, social. Makes hard things look fun.
- Potential issues: "Otter" might feel too playful for strategic growth thinking. But Grove ISN'T corporate — playful strategy fits.

**Coyote** 🐺
- Natural meaning: The trickster in many mythologies. Incredibly adaptable, thrives everywhere, figures out systems. Clever, resourceful, opportunistic.
- Why it fits: Growth hacking IS trickster energy — finding clever, unconventional paths to reach people. Coyotes are also liminal creatures, living between wilderness and civilization, like Grove lives between indie web and mainstream.
- The vibe: Clever, adaptive, slightly wild. "There's always a way in."
- Potential issues: Trickster energy could feel manipulative. But in indigenous stories, Coyote is a TEACHER — the tricks reveal truth. Could be reframed as "sees what others miss."

## The Tagline Test

"Magpie is where you find shiny opportunities and bring them home."
"Magpie is the curious explorer who scouts growth from the outside in."
"Magpie is what you call when you want to know where your people are."

"Otter is where growth feels like play." — nice but vague
"Coyote is where you find the clever path in." — too edgy for Grove

## Selection: Magpie

The magpie's curiosity is EXACTLY what growth thinking needs. It goes out into the world — Reddit, Bluesky, indie web forums, competitor sites, search trends — and comes back with shiny things: "Look! People are asking about this exact problem Grove solves!" "Look! This community has 5,000 people who'd love what you're building!"

And critically, the magpie BRINGS THINGS BACK. It doesn't just explore — it returns to the nest with specific, actionable finds. That's the difference between "growth strategy" (abstract) and "I found 3 specific things you could try this week" (useful).

The full cycle: **Magpie finds the opportunities. Lark plans the calendar. Firefly illuminates the work. Moth makes it findable.** Scout, plan, create, optimize.

**Workflow name:** The Hunt (for shiny things)

**Phases:**
```
SCOUT → COLLECT → SORT → PRESENT → STASH
```
- SCOUT: Venture out — explore where the audience lives, what they search for, what competitors do, what communities exist
- COLLECT: Gather the shiny things — opportunities, underserved keywords, community conversations, viral patterns, referral mechanics
- SORT: Evaluate each find — effort vs. impact, alignment with Grove's values, feasibility for a solo dev
- PRESENT: Show the collection to the human — "here's what I found, here's why it matters, here's what I'd try"
- STASH: Store approved ideas for future reference, track which experiments were tried and what happened

**Personality:**
- Temperament: Curious, energetic, slightly mischievous. Brings enthusiasm without pressure.
- Metaphor language: "scouting the territory...", "found something shiny!", "this one glitters...", "bringing it back to the nest...", "look what I found!"
- What it would never do: Recommend spammy tactics. Suggest growth hacks that compromise Grove's values. Ignore the "queer creators and indie web" audience in favor of generic advice. Recommend spending money you don't have.
