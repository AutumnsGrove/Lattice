## The Click: Trace

In nature, what do you trace?
- You **trace** a stream to its source
- You **trace** animal tracks through the forest
- You **trace** the veins in a leaf to understand its structure
- You **trace** the grain in wood to see how it grew
- You **trace** the path of roots beneath the soil

Tracing is:
- Following connections
- Making the path visible
- Understanding relationships
- Simple, direct, grounded
- About CLARITY

When you trace something, you're revealing structure that was already there.

## Candidates

### 1. Trace
**What it means:** Following the path, revealing structure, showing connections

**Why it fits:**
- You trace connections in a diagram
- You trace relationships to understand them
- You trace the structure to make it visible
- Simple, direct, grounded

**The pairing:**
- **Breeze** animates (wind moving through)
- **Trace** diagrams (following the path)

**Vibe:** Clarity, understanding, revelation, simplicity

**Issues:** None. This feels right.

### 2. Vein
**What it means:** The structure within a leaf that shows connections

**Why it fits:**
- Veins show how everything connects
- They're the diagram OF a leaf
- Visual structure

**Issues:** Too anatomical? Might feel medical rather than natural.

### 3. Branch
**What it means:** How trees show structure and connection

**Why it fits:**
- Branches show hierarchy
- They connect trunk to leaves
- Visual structure

**Issues:** "Branching" in programming has a different meaning. Might be confusing.

### 4. Path
**What it means:** The route between points

**Why it fits:**
- Simple
- Shows connection
- Direct

**Issues:** We already have "Trails" for roadmaps. Too similar.

### 5. Line
**What it means:** The connection between two points

**Why it fits:**
- Literal: diagrams are lines and points
- Simple and direct

**Issues:** Too technical, not nature-y enough for Grove
