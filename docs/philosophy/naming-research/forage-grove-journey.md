## Breaking Down the Action

What's happening here?

1. **Input**: vague feeling, project vision, constraints
2. **Process**: AI-powered intelligent search
3. **Output**: curated list of available, fitting domains
4. **Result**: you claim one (ownership)

This is:
- **Searching** (looking for something specific)
- **Intelligent** (AI does the work)
- **Selective** (not everything, just what fits)
- **Naming** (finding the RIGHT name)
- **Claiming** (you take it home, it's yours)

## What in Nature is "Assisted Discovery"?

Things that help you find what you couldn't alone:

| Helper | What it finds | How |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| Truffle pig | Rare mushrooms | Scent |
| Hunting dog | Game birds | Scent + pointing |
| Falcon | Prey | Speed + sight |
| Divining rod | Water | Mysterious sense |
| Scout | Safe paths | Going ahead |
| Tracker | Game | Reading signs |

The key insight: **You don't do the finding. Something else does.**

Foraging is YOU doing the work.
This is... something else working FOR you.

## The Naming Focus

Here's what I keep coming back to:

Domain discovery is about **finding a name**.

Not finding food (forage).
Not finding paths (scout).
Not finding water (dowse).

Finding... what your project is CALLED.

What in nature is about names?

```
    Naming in nature:

    - Species get Latin names (taxonomy)
    - Places get named after features
    - Animals name their territory
    - Birds have calls that identify them

    What identifies something in a forest?

    - Its species (oak, maple, pine)
    - Its call (bird songs)
    - Its scent (animal marking)
    - Its place (the big rock, the bent tree)
```
