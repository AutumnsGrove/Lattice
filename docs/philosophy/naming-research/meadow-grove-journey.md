## What IS a Social Feed?

Let me think about what this thing actually IS.

Traditional social media is:
- Algorithmic (shows you what keeps you engaged)
- Metric-driven (public follower counts, likes, retweets)
- Performance-based (optimize for visibility)
- Addictive by design
- A town square... but a hostile one

What we're building is:
- Chronological (what's new, in order)
- Private metrics (only authors see their reactions)
- Discovery-focused (find new voices)
- Opt-in (you choose to step into it)
- Anti-viral (no mechanisms to "go viral")
- Connection without competition

This isn't a town square. It's not a stage.

It's more like... a potluck? A circle of chairs? A...

## Does "Meadow" Capture What We Need?

Let me check each aspect:

### OPEN (visible, public)
Yes. A meadow is open. The canopy parts. You see others.
Your posts in the meadow are visible to the community.

### SUNNY (warm, welcoming)
Yes. Meadows are bright, inviting.
Wildflowers. Butterflies. A pleasant place to be.

### COMMUNAL (gathering space)
Yes. Animals gather in meadows. Deer graze.
It's a natural meeting point.

### OPT-IN (you choose to step into it)
Hmm. This is where it gets interesting.
You don't accidentally end up in a meadow.
You have to walk there. Leave your tree's shadow.
Yes, I think this works.

### FEED-BASED (chronological discovery)
A meadow doesn't really convey "chronological."
But does it need to? The name doesn't have to describe
the implementation. "Meadow" is the place.
How it works is just... how meadows work here.

### ANTI-VIRAL (no public metrics)
Does a meadow feel anti-viral? Anti-algorithm?
Hmm. A meadow is just... natural.
There's no forced amplification in a meadow.
No one's BOOSTING you to the front.
You're just there, and so are others.
That feels right, actually.

### ANTI-HIERARCHY
In a meadow, is there hierarchy?
No one's on a stage. No podium.
Just... grass. Everyone at ground level.
That works.

## But Wait. What Am I Missing?

Let me think about what "Meadow" might NOT capture.

### The Intimacy

A meadow can be large. Wide open. Impersonal.
But our social layer is meant to feel... small?
Cozy? Like you know the people here?

Hmm. Is the Grove community going to be huge?
Probably not at first. Maybe never.
This is a niche. Writers who want something different.

A small meadow feels right for a small community.

### The Shelter

The grove is about shelter. Shade. Protection.
A meadow is exposed. No canopy.

Is that a problem?

Maybe not. The posts you share in the meadow are
the ones you WANT to be seen. You're choosing exposure.
Your blog is still your shaded tree.
Meadow is just where you go when you want sunlight.

### The "Feed" Connotation

"Meadow" doesn't sound like a feed.
But neither does "Instagram" or "Twitter."
The name doesn't have to describe function.

## Alternative Directions

Let me explore some alternatives anyway.

### GLEN
A small valley between hills. Sheltered but open.
More intimate than a meadow.

"glen.grove.place"

A glen is:
- Smaller than a meadow
- Protected by surrounding terrain
- Often has a stream
- Quiet, tucked-away
- A gathering place in folktales

Does glen feel too obscure? Too Scottish?
Hmm. It's a real word, but less universally understood.

### DELL
Similar to glen. A small valley.

"dell.grove.place"

A dell is:
- Poetic (Farmer in the Dell)
- Small, wooded valley
- Sheltered
- A bit archaic

Dell feels more sheltered than meadow.
But also more obscure.

### CLEARING
Already taken for the status page.
And "Clearing" is about visibility/status, not community.

### GLADE
A glade is an open space in a forest.
Very similar to meadow, actually.

"glade.grove.place"

A glade is:
- An opening in woodland
- Sunlit
- Often grassy
- Magical connotation (fairy glades)
- More intimate than meadow?

Glade feels more magical. More intentional.
A meadow can be big. A glade feels smaller, special.

### GROVE (itself)
Can't use this. Grove is the whole ecosystem.

### HOLLOW
A hollow is a small valley or depression.
But "hollow" also means empty, which is bad.

### CIRCLE
"The Circle" - a gathering of people.
But this doesn't fit the nature theme.

### ROUND
Like a dance circle? "The Round"?
Too abstract.

## My Conclusion

**Meadow is the right name.**

I've walked through the alternatives:
- **Glade** - More intimate, more magical, but also more obscure.
  Meadow is more universally understood.
- **Glen/Dell** - Too obscure, too location-specific.
- **Clearing** - Already taken (status page).
- **The Green/Commons** - Too civilized, not wild enough.
- **Hollow** - Sounds empty, wrong connotation.

Meadow captures:
- The opening in the forest (community space)
- The sunlight (visibility, warmth)
- The anti-algorithm (no canopy filtering the light)
- The gentleness (wildflowers, not concrete)
- The opt-in nature (you walk there from your tree)
- The equality (everyone at ground level)

The existing naming doc entry is genuinely good.
It doesn't need to change.
