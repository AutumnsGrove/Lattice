## What ARE analytics in this world?

Let me think about what analytics actually *do*:

- They count (how many people read this?)
- They track over time (growth, decline, patterns)
- They reveal patterns (what resonates?)
- They're for YOU, not the audience
- They help you understand yourself/your work

In regular analytics:
- Public view counts = anxiety machines
- Real-time = dopamine hits
- Leaderboards = competition
- Metrics = performing for an audience

In Grove analytics:
- Private = just for you
- Delayed = no dopamine, reflection instead
- Personal = understanding your own growth
- Quiet = no comparisons, no anxiety

## The Rings Metaphor

Tree rings are what you see when you cut a tree open.

Each ring = one year.
- Wide ring = good year, plenty of rain, lots of growth
- Narrow ring = hard year, drought, struggle
- Dark marks = fire scars
- Irregular patterns = storms, stress, recovery

Rings are:
- **Internal** - you don't see them from outside
- **Historical** - they record time passing
- **Private** - only visible when you look closely
- **Honest** - they don't lie about what happened
- **Cumulative** - each year adds to the story

*Count the rings of a tree and you learn its story.*

This feels... right?

## The Moment of Clarity

I think **Rings** is right.

Here's why it works:

1. **The metaphor is exact**: Tree rings record seasons of growth. Private analytics record your growth as a writer.

2. **Privacy is built in**: You can only see rings by looking INSIDE your tree. No one else can see them.

3. **Delay is natural**: You can't count a ring until the season is over. This is anti-real-time by nature.

4. **No comparison**: Your rings are YOURS. You can't compare your rings to another tree's. They're incompatible. This prevents leaderboard anxiety.

5. **It tells a story**: Rings show good years and hard years. Wide growth and narrow survival. It's honest, not gamified.

6. **The word is warm**: "Rings" sounds gentle. Not clinical like "analytics" or "metrics" or "stats."

Just private insights about your own growth: who's reading, what resonates, how your garden is growing over time. Delayed by design, reflective by nature. Your rings are yours alone.

The name was already found. This journey confirmed it.

---

## Possible Enhancements

If we wanted to add depth:

1. **Ring View** - the dashboard page could be called "Ring View"
2. **Seasons** - time periods could be "seasons" not months
3. **Growth Rings** - the charts showing upward trends
4. **Wide Ring / Narrow Ring** - good months vs quiet months

The vocabulary around Rings is rich:
- "A wide ring this season" = you grew a lot
- "A narrow ring" = quiet period (not failure, just rest)
- "Dark marks" = significant events, turning points
- "The heartwood at the center" = your core identity through it all
