> *"Light enters plain and emerges transformed."*

- Glassmorphism surfaces (translucent, blurred backgrounds)

- Weather effects (snow, petals, leaves)
- The overall aesthetic that makes every page feel like a **place**

This is the visual LANGUAGE of Grove.
The ATMOSPHERE.
The thing that makes you feel like you're IN the forest, not just looking at a website.

## What Creates Atmosphere in a Forest?

Not a prism. A forest doesn't have prisms.

A forest has:
- **Canopy** (the overhead coverage that filters light)
- **Dappled light** (patches of sun through leaves)
- **Mist** (morning atmosphere, soft blur)
- **Gloaming** (the magical light at twilight)
- **Weather** (seasonal changes in the air)

These are all ATMOSPHERIC. WARM. ORGANIC.

## Coming Back to Dapple

The more I walk through this, the more **Dapple** feels right.

Here's why:

### 1. It's specifically about light through trees

Glassmorphism is about transparency and blurâ€”seeing hints of what's behind.
Dappled light is about hints of sun through leaves.
Same concept, but ORGANIC.

### 2. It creates patterns

A design system is fundamentally about PATTERNS.
Dappled light creates organic patterns on the forest floor.
Not grid-based. Not geometric. Natural variation with underlying harmony.

### 3. It's warm

Dappled light feels warm. Cozy. Inviting.
A prism is cold, scientific, precise.
Dapple is "midnight tea shop warmth."

### 4. It changes with seasons

In summer: dense dappling through full canopy
In autumn: golden light through thinning leaves
In winter: stark light through bare branches
In spring: soft light through new growth

The design system IS seasonal. Dapple captures that.

### 5. It's unmistakably forest

Dappled light is A GROVE THING.
You can't have dappled light without trees.
It roots the design system in the forest metaphor.

## The Verdict

I think **Dapple** is the right name.

But I want to be honest about the tradeoffs:

**Keeping Prism:**
- Less disruption (it's already named)
- The cathedral metaphor IS beautiful
- Connects explicitly to glassmorphism
- More "techy" which might appeal to developers

**Changing to Dapple:**
- More nature-rooted (fits the grove metaphor)
- Warmer, more inviting
- About BEING in the forest, not about optical physics
- The word itself evokes the feeling we want
- New adoption effort across codebase
