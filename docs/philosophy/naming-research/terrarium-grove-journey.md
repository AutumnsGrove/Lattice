## What Am I Naming?

A creative canvas where you:
- Drag and drop nature components (trees, flowers, creatures, fireflies)
- Arrange them into scenes
- Export those scenes as decorations for your blog
- Header decorations, sidebar ornaments, footer scenes, backgrounds

It's like... digital bonsai meets scrapbooking meets MySpace customization.
But within Grove's curated nature palette.

---

## The Current Name: Terrarium

A terrarium is a sealed world under glass. Moss, tiny plants, small stones. You arrange it. You close it. You watch it grow (or remain static).

**What's RIGHT about Terrarium:**
- Contained, curated, intentional
- A miniature world you design
- Every element placed with purpose
- There's something precious about it

**What feels WRONG about Terrarium:**
- A terrarium is *sealed*. Closed off. Separate.
- You look at it from outside, through glass
- It's an artificial environment
- It's static once created
- The glass barrier feels... cold?

But more importantly: a terrarium is something you *observe*.
This tool is something you *use to create*.

Is there a difference between the thing you make and the place where you make it?
