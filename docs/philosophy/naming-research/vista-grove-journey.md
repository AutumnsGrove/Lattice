## Walking Through...

I am the grove keeper. Not a tree. Not a visitor.
I'm the one who tends this forest.

The users don't see me. They just know
their trees are healthy, their roots are fed,
their leaves change colors when they should.

But I need to know when something's wrong.
Before they know. Before it hurts them.

Where do I go to see the forest's health?

---

## What IS Infrastructure Monitoring in Forest Terms?

Let me think about what this thing actually does:

**It watches constantly.** Never sleeps. Eyes always open.

**It sees beneath the surface.** Not the pretty foliage,
but the roots, the soil moisture, the hidden rot.

**It sends warnings.** When a tree is stressed,
when the soil turns acidic, when pests approach.

**It remembers.** Ninety days of forest history.
Was this tree always struggling? Is this new?

**It measures the invisible.** Latency. Error rates.
Things no visitor would ever see or understand.

## What Vista Captures:

- Elevated perspective (above the day-to-day)
- Comprehensive view (seeing everything)
- Assessment (understanding the state)
- Distance (removed from the individual trees)

---

## What Vista Might NOT Capture:

- **The watching** - A vista is a place, not an activity.
  You visit a vista. You don't man a vista.

- **The alerting** - Vistas don't warn you.
  They just ARE. They're passive.

- **The keeper role** - Anyone can visit a vista.
  It doesn't imply stewardship.

- **The continuous nature** - A vista is a moment.
  You take in the view, then you leave.

## Thinking About the ALERTING Function

This is important. Vista doesn't alert.
A vista just IS. It shows you things if you look.

But infrastructure monitoring is ACTIVE.
It reaches out and taps you on the shoulder:

"Hey. Something's wrong with that tree."
"The soil pH is dropping."
"A worker fell silent."

In forest terms, what sends alerts?

- **Smoke** - Rising, visible from far away (danger signal)
- **Bird calls** - Warning calls when predators approach
- **The underground network** - Trees signal stress through mycelium

But wait... we already have Mycelium (the MCP server).
And smoke/bird calls feel like individual signals, not systems.

## Coming Back to Vista

The more I think about it...

**Vista** works because:

1. It's the keeper's viewpoint, not the visitor's
2. The name implies comprehensive vision
3. "Vista" sounds warm, natural, grove-like
4. It pairs nicely with "Clearing" (both are about seeing)
5. The domain feels right: vista.grove.place

**The gap I was worried about (alerting)**...
Can be handled in the description and features,
not necessarily the name itself.

After all, Ivy doesn't literally climb anything.
Amber doesn't literally fossilize files.
The metaphor carries the essence, not every function.

After walking through the grove, I think **Vigil**
captures what infrastructure monitoring IS better
than Vista does.

Vista = a beautiful view
Vigil = someone is watching, caring, ready

Infrastructure monitoring isn't about enjoying a view.
It's about keeping watch. Being ready. Caring enough
to stay awake through the night so users don't have to.

**Vigil** - `vigil.grove.place`

> A vigil is purposeful watchfulness. Through the night,
> while the grove sleeps, someone stays awakeâ€”not from

> costsâ€”everything visible at a glance. When something stirs
