## The Question: What IS Content Moderation?

Before I can name it, I need to understand what it actually IS.

In a platform without moderation:
- Spam floods the comments
- Harassment drives people away
- The space becomes hostile
- The community withers

In a platform with moderation:
- Harmful content is filtered out
- Conversations stay healthy
- People feel safe to speak
- The community flourishes

So moderation is... protection? Health maintenance? Gardening?

- *"Every rose has thorns for protection"*
- Natural defense mechanism
- Part of the plant, not external armor
- Thorns deter, they don't attack

## But Wait... What IS Moderation Really Doing?

Thorns are reactive. You touch them, you get pricked.

But moderation is:
- **Active** â€” it DOES something, constantly scanning
- **Context-aware** â€” understands nuance, not just keywords
- **Invisible ideally** â€” you don't notice it working
- **About community health** â€” not just defense
- **Privacy-first** â€” never stores, never trains on content
- **Caring** â€” it's tending, not just protecting

Does "thorn" capture the ACTIVE nature?
Does it capture the INVISIBLE quality?
Does it capture the CARING aspect?

Hmm.
