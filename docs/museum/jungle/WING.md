# The Jungle

> *The animal skills, the tooling, and the craft of building things that build things.*

---

## What You're Looking At

This wing is about velocity. Not the "move fast and break things" kind. The kind where good tooling compounds into momentum, where 72 specialized skills orchestrate complex work, and where custom Go binaries turn eight commits per issue into two.

The Jungle is where the grove tends itself. Every animal has a role. Every tool was built because the alternative was slower, messier, or less joyful. This is the craft of building things that build things.

---

## Exhibits

### The Menagerie
72 animal skills organized by role. Builders (Elephant, Beaver, Frog). Investigators (Mole, Bloodhound, Raccoon). Architects (Eagle, Swan, Crow). Protectors (Turtle, Hawk, Spider). Guides (Robin, Deer, Druid). Each skill is a specialist, dispatched with fresh context, focused on one job.

### The Conductor
The orchestration pattern. How Gatherings coordinate multiple skills into a single workflow. Fresh-context subagent dispatch. Gate verification between stages. The pattern that turned "build a feature" from a monologue into an ensemble.

### GW: Grove Wrap
The infrastructure CLI. A Go binary that wraps git, CI, secrets, deployments, and more behind a consistent interface. The tool that changed the game. How it went from convenience to indispensable.

### GF: Grove Find
Codebase search reimagined. A Go binary with hybrid search that finds what you need in ~40ms across thousands of files. The tool that makes exploration instant.

### Glimpse
Development snapshots. A way to capture moments during the build process, preserving context that would otherwise be lost. The photographer of the jungle.

### The Craft
How tooling compounds into velocity. The story arc from 8 commits per issue to 2.2. What happens when you invest in your own workflow. Why building your own tools isn't premature optimization when the tools are this good.
