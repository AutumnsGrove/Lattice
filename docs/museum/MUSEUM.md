# Welcome to the Grove Museum

> *A guided tour through how this forest grows.*

---

## What You're Looking At

This is the story of Grove, told through the systems that make it work.

You're standing at the entrance to a museum. Not the dusty kind with ropes and "do not touch" signs. This is the kind where you can peek behind every curtain, ask why everything exists, and leave understanding something new.

Grove is an aspen grove. Not a metaphor bolted on after the fact, but a discovery: aspens are clonal colonies. One root system, many trunks. They share nutrients underground. They're resilient not through armor, but through connection. That's what this platform is. Lattice is the root system. Every blog, every tenant, every feature grows from the same living network.

This museum shows you how that grove was planted, piece by piece.

### [1. The Aspen Wing](./aspen/WING.md)

Why this exists. Why it was built this way.

This is the heart of the museum. Not architecture, not code, but values. Privacy-first. Solarpunk. Queer. Indie web. You own your content. You are your words. The story of someone who needed a space and ended up building an entire world.

**Exhibits:**
- The Origin Story (from personal blog to platform)
- The Values (privacy, ownership, community without algorithms)
- The Aspen Identity (why aspens, why clonal colonies, why connection)
- The Predecessor (the AutumnsGrove Museum, where it all began)

---

### [2. The Philosophy Wing](./philosophy/WING.md)

How Grove names things, makes decisions, and builds with intention.

Names aren't branding. They're world-building. Every service has a nature metaphor that genuinely fits. Each name was discovered through a walk through the forest, not a brainstorming session with sticky notes. This wing preserves the meticulous process behind every decision.

**Exhibits:**
- The Naming Ritual (how names are discovered, not invented)
- The Walks Gallery (56+ naming journeys, organized and accessible)
- The Ecosystem Map (60+ services, each with its story)
- The Design Language (warm, introspective, queer, unapologetically building)

---

### [3. The Architecture Wing](./architecture/WING.md)

How Grove is built at the infrastructure level.

One deployment serves unlimited blogs. One engine prevents duplication. Cloudflare primitives compose together like instruments in an orchestra. The Infra SDK abstracts it all.

**Exhibits:**
- The Foundation (multi-tenant architecture)
- The Engine Room (engine-first pattern)
- The Loom (Durable Objects coordination)
- The Cloud Garden (Cloudflare primitives)
- The Infra SDK (GroveDatabase, GroveStorage, GroveKV)

---

### [4. The Nature Wing](./nature/WING.md)

The visual and emotional language of Grove.

Mathematically-driven SVG components create a living forest. Five seasons shift the mood. Glass surfaces layer over organic backgrounds. Gossamer weaves ASCII effects into translucent cards. Prism refracts design tokens, colors, and icons into seasonal palettes.

**Exhibits:**
- The Forest (trees, creatures, weather effects)
- The Seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter, Midnight)
- The Glass Garden (glassmorphism design)
- The Typography Gallery (accessible fonts)
- Gossamer (ASCII art effects, domain warping, 8 presets)
- Prism (design tokens, colors, icons, seasonal depth)

---

### [5. The Trust Wing](./trust/WING.md)

Authentication, security, and identity.

Login should feel safe, not surveilled. Sessions refresh quietly. Protection happens without frustration. Heartwood is the name we gave to Grove's core of trust.

**Exhibits:**
- The Heartwood (OAuth and identity)
- The Session Gallery (remembering who you are)
- The Security Checkpoint (protection patterns)

---

### [6. The Data Wing](./data/WING.md)

How information flows and persists.

Posts travel from editor to database to cache to browser. Tenant isolation keeps your data yours. Amber manages files. Type-safe helpers prevent the scary stuff.

**Exhibits:**
- The Filing Cabinet (D1 database patterns)
- The Quick-Lookup Shelf (KV caching)
- The Media Vault (R2 storage)
- Amber (unified storage management)
- The Query Builders (type-safe helpers)

---

### [7. The Personalization Wing](./personalization/WING.md)

Making each blog feel like home.

Feature flags have Grove vocabulary: Graft means enable, Prune means disable. Curios bring old-web energy: guestbooks, hit counters, custom cursors. Blazes mark content with meaning. Your space should feel like yours.

**Exhibits:**
- The Grafts Exhibit (feature flags)
- The Curios Collection (old-web personalization)
- The Theme Workshop (Foliage customization)
- Blazes (content markers)

---

### [8. The Community Wing](./community/WING.md)

The social layer and shared spaces.

The Meadow is where blogs can share posts to a community feed. The Landing welcomes visitors to grove.place. The Clearing shows system health with transparency.

**Exhibits:**
- The Meadow (community feed)
- The Canopy (opt-in wanderer directory)
- The Landing (welcome experience)
- The Clearing (status page)

---

### [9. The AI Wing](./ai/WING.md)

Privacy-first AI that serves creators, not surveillance.

Grove's AI features have zero data retention. Thorn moderates content through behavioral analysis before AI ever touches it. Reverie transforms natural language into site configuration. Lumen abstracts AI providers so no single vendor owns your intelligence. Songbird protects against prompt injection.

**Exhibits:**
- Thorn (content moderation, behavioral + AI layers)
- Reverie (AI configuration pipeline, "make my site cozy")
- Lumen (provider abstraction, tool-calling, zero lock-in)
- Songbird (prompt injection protection)

---

### [10. The Jungle](./jungle/WING.md)

The animal skills, the tooling, and the craft of building things that build things.

72 animal skills. A conductor orchestration pattern with fresh-context subagent dispatch. GW and GF, custom Go binaries that transformed development velocity from 8 commits per issue to 2. This is where you see how the grove tends itself.

**Exhibits:**
- The Menagerie (72 skills across categories)
- The Conductor (orchestration pattern)
- GW: Grove Wrap (infrastructure CLI)
- GF: Grove Find (codebase search)
- The Craft (how tooling compounds into velocity)

## Before You Go

This museum exists because documentation should be hospitable.

When you enter a codebase, you're a visitor unfamiliar with its architecture and decisions. Most codebases hand you a catalog and wish you luck. We'd rather walk alongside you, explaining why things exist before diving into how they work.

This project started as a personal blog. It became an engine. The engine became a platform. The platform became a world. And the world turned out to be an aspen grove, quietly sharing roots beneath the surface, resilient through connection.

Every exhibit tells a story. Every pattern has a reason. Every decision has a lesson.

Take your time. Ask questions. The forest will still be here.
